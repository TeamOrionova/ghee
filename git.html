The Origin

Why Gir Cow A2 Ghee?

Our ghee is made exclusively from the milk of the Gir cow, a beloved native breed of Gujarat. Unlike regular ghee made from hybrid buffalo or crossbred cows, Gir milk is rich in A2 Beta-casein protein.

This protein is gentle on the stomach, aids digestion, and is known to boost immunity. We strictly follow the Bilona method—curdling milk into curd, hand-churning it to separate butter, and then slow-cooking it over fire. This preserves the natural aroma and nutrients.

Traditional Method

The Bilona Process

01

Fresh Milk to Curd

Whole A2 milk is boiled, cooled, and cultured to set into thick, nutrient-rich curd overnight.

02

Wooden Churning

The curd is hand-churned using a traditional wooden churner (Bilona) to extract pure white butter (Makkhan).

03

Slow Heating

The butter is heated on a low flame until water evaporates, leaving behind golden A2 Ghee with a rich nutty aroma.
